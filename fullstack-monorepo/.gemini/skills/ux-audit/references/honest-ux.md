# Honest & Ethical UX Audit Skill

**Sources:** smashingmagazine.com/2016/10/how-to-build-honest-uis-and-help-users-make-better-decisions

---

## When to invoke this skill

Invoke this skill when auditing:
- **Join page** — subscriber opt-in (what are they agreeing to?)
- **Unsubscribe page** — opt-out flow (easy? friction-free?)
- **Mailing Lists** — subscription management (is it clear what a subscriber is being added to?)
- **Settings** — scheduler, scraping behaviour, data decisions
- **Invite flow** — what does the invite email tell a recipient before they click?

### Do not hide friction or costs in tooltips

If there are conditions attached to the subscription (e.g., only certain lists are receiving content, or the newsletter is published irregularly), state this near the submit action — not buried in a tooltip or fine print only.

## Unsubscribe UX — Remove Dark Patterns

The unsubscribe flow must be:
- **One click or at most one confirmation** — not a multi-page survey, not re-engagement tricks
- **Immediately effective** — no "Your request will be processed within 10 business days"
- **Honest about what happens** — "You have been unsubscribed from Romania Digest. You will receive no further emails."
- **No guilt-based messaging** — avoid copy like "Are you sure? You'll miss out on great content."

## Algorithmic and Automated Decision Transparency

The app runs automated scraping and AI-powered summarisation. Users interacting with AI-summarised articles should know:

- **Article Queue**: are AI-generated summaries labelled as AI-generated? Editors should know they are reviewing a machine summary, not the original author's words.
- **Scraper**: when did the last scrape run? Does the Dashboard show when articles were collected and from which sources?
- **Scheduler**: in Settings, is it clear what the scheduler does, when it runs, and what happens if it is disabled?

## User Control and Easy Opt-Out

**Principle:** Users must be able to undo or opt out of automated behaviours easily.

- Scheduler on/off toggle must be prominent and labelled with what it does — not hidden deep in settings
- "Run scrape manually" must be available even when scheduler is enabled (for testing and on-demand use)
- Bulk actions (bulk delete, bulk send) must have a confirmation step that shows the scope ("Delete 23 articles?")
- Any action that cannot be undone (send edition, delete subscriber, revoke invite) must warn the user and require confirmation — preferably stating the irreversibility explicitly: "This cannot be undone."

## Consent and Data Transparency

- The invite system collects subscriber email addresses. The app stores these. Subscribers have a right to know this when they join.
- The Unsubscribe flow is the primary mechanism for data removal. Confirm it removes the subscriber record or at minimum stops all communication.
- If the app integrates with external mailing providers (SMTP relay, Mailpit) — this should be transparent to admins in the Settings UI.

## Anti-Patterns to Flag Immediately

| Pattern | Severity | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Unsubscribe buried or multi-step | Critical | Must be maximum 1-click from any email or 1 step on the unsubscribe page |
| "Are you sure you want to miss out?" guilt copy | Major | Manipulative retention copy on the unsubscribe page |
| No description of newsletter on Join page | Major | Subscriber cannot make an informed decision |
| AI summary not labelled | Major | Editor believes they are reading the author's words, not an AI summary |
| Scheduler behaviour not explained in Settings | Minor | Admin cannot understand what will happen when they enable the scheduler |
| Credentials/tokens visible in plain text | Critical | SMTP password, API keys, subscriber tokens must be masked by default |

## Content Lifecycle Transparency Additions (GOV.UK)

Reference sources: gov.uk/guidance/content-design/content-maintenance, gov.uk/guidance/content-design/gov-uk-content-retention-and-withdrawal-archiving-policy

- Make lifecycle states explicit in authoring and archive views (draft, scheduled, sent, archived, withdrawn).
- For withdrawal or unsend-like actions, require clear rationale and replacement guidance.
- Show exact retention/archive scope before commit actions.
- Include recurring maintenance checks for stale links, outdated events, and deprecated edition content.

---

## UI Decision Guide Integration

Canonical reference: `UI_DECISION_GUIDE.md`

- Apply decision-first clarity to trust-sensitive actions: state, consequence, and next action must be explicit.
- Keep consent and transparency copy tied to real user decisions, not implementation details.
- Ensure lifecycle and withdrawal messaging is actionable at the point of decision.
